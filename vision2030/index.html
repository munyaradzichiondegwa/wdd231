Zimbabwe Vision 2030

Towards an Upper Middle-Income Economy by 2030: Inclusive Growth, Innovation, and Resilience

Zimbabwe's journey towards Vision 2030 is being implemented through a series of strategic development plans. The initial phase was the Transitional Stabilisation Programme (TSP), which focused on stabilising the economy and laying the groundwork for future growth. Building on the TSP, the National Development Strategy 1 (NDS1) (2021-2025) is currently underway, prioritizing economic growth and stability. NDS2 will follow, continuing the trajectory towards achieving the goals of Vision 2030.

Key Programs

TSP 2018-2020

Transitional Stabilisation Programme: Laying the Foundation for Economic Recovery and Sustainable Growth.

The TSP addressed urgent economic challenges through fiscal consolidation, monetary policy reforms, and structural adjustments. Key priorities included stabilising the exchange rate, reducing inflation, and creating a more attractive investment climate.

Learn More About TSP

NDS1 2021-2025

National Development Strategy 1: Accelerating Economic Development Towards Vision 2030.

NDS1 prioritizes macroeconomic stability, inclusive growth, value chain development, and strengthening social infrastructure. Aims for an average GDP growth rate above 5%.

Explore NDS1

NDS2 2026-2030

National Development Strategy 2: Towards a Prosperous and Modern Zimbabwe.

NDS2 is expected to prioritize infrastructure development, technological advancement, and human capital development to drive sustainable economic growth and improve the quality of life for all citizens.

Discover NDS2
